The Old Kingdom

This is the earliest period of the Empire that was Ancient Egypt (c. 2686–2181 BCE)

The Old Kingdom is often called the "Age of the Pyramids," saw the construction of iconic monuments like the Great Pyramid of Giza under pharaohs such as Khufu, with a centralized government, booming trade, and advancements in art and architecture, though it ended in instability due to weak rulers, climate change, and famine.
