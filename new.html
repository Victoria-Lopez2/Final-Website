The New Kingdom

The Age of the Pharaohs (c. 1550–1070 BCE)

The New Kingdom represented Egypt's imperial zenith, with warrior-pharaohs like Hatshepsut, Thutmose III, Akhenaten, Tutankhamun, and Ramesses II conquering vast territories from Nubia to Syria, building colossal temples at Karnak and Abu Simbel, amassing unprecedented wealth, and pioneering religious reforms, before declining due to internal strife, foreign incursions, and economic strain.
